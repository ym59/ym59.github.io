Hi! My name is Yash.

Computers have always been a source of fascination for me since my childhood, and this fascination has only grown stronger with time. As a result, I pursued a career in Bioinformatics, which combines my interests in both biology and computing. I completed my Masters in Bioinformatics from Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi in 2020, which provided me with a comprehensive understanding of the field. Prior to this, I completed my Bachelors in Microbiology from the University of Delhi in 2018, which laid the foundation for my interest in biological sciences.
Currently, I am a PhD Scholar under the supervision of Dr. Md. Imtaiyaz Hassan, focusing on leveraging Artificial Intelligence to tackle complex problems in computational biology. My work involves a diverse array of tasks, ranging from data analysis to the creation of AI models that aid in the discovery and development of new therapeutic agents. In addition to my work in the research lab, I am also actively involved in the scientific community, participating in conferences and workshops related to Bioinformatics. I am passionate about using computational tools and techniques to advance our understanding of the biological world, and I am always eager to collaborate with others who share the same vision. Ultimately, my goal is to make meaningful contributions to the field of Computational Biology and to help drive scientific progress in this exciting and rapidly evolving field.
Beyond my professional interests, I have a deep passion for astronomy and history. These hobbies have always captivated my curiosity, and I find great joy in exploring the mysteries of the universe and delving into the rich tapestry of human civilization. To share my fascination with others, I plan to incorporate a blog into this website where I can post about various astronomical phenomena and historical events that intrigue me. Through these posts, I hope to engage with like-minded individuals and contribute to the broader understanding and appreciation of these subjects. Whether it's a recent astronomical discovery or a lesser-known historical event, I aim to explore and share research that I find interesting and thought-provoking.

Projects



  • InstaDock
    A front-end graphical user interface written in Python to perform molecular docking-based virtual high-throughput screening


  • PyPan
    It serves as a universal platform to analyse protein sequences, structure, and their properties.


  • Protein Function Prediction
    Prediction of Gene Ontology (GO) terms for protein sequences, divided into three subontologies: Molecular Function (MF), Biological Process (BP), and Cellular Component (CC).


  • Heart Failure Prediction
    A study of integrating multiple models to compare predictions for heart failure, based on dataset provided containing 12 attributes including age, sex, chest pain type, Cholestrol levels and Resting Blood pressure.


  • Monet style photo Generator
    A Generative Artificial Network to generate Monet style photos, based on dataset provided containing 300 Monet style images and 7000+ realistic images.


  • Modelling Method Advisory Tool (MMAT)
    Django framework based python webtool that can help researchers accurately decide the protein modelling method to employ for the best efficiency.
